# Spec-vs-harness audit — `hmm_validation_v0`

This document is a factual inventory of differences between the formal specification in §4.1 of `docs/layers/layer_2/research/Insider Divergence Detection as Bayesian HMM Spec with Covariate-Dependent Emission.md` and the executed harness at `run.py`. Severity is reported per row; the v0.2 Layer 2 design document (§11 limitations subsection) cites this audit as the basis for its honest gap statement.

Severity legend: `EQUIV` — the harness is functionally equivalent to the spec; `SIMPLIFIED` — the harness is a tractable approximation that should be expanded in v1; `MISSING` — the harness omits a spec element entirely; `DEVIATION` — the harness contradicts the spec.

## Severity rollup

The audit identifies 32 spec elements. Of these: 11 are EQUIV, 9 are SIMPLIFIED, 10 are MISSING, and 2 are DEVIATION. The MISSING and DEVIATION rows concentrate on the action-emission decomposition (size, code, 10b5-1 flag, informative-absence parameterization), the hierarchical Type-II ML structure (outer loop, role-level pooling, transition kernel), and the diagnostics infrastructure (JSONL output, covariate-absorption check, Fisher info eigenvalue, cold-start logic, per-executive identifiability flag).

The SIMPLIFIED rows concentrate on the narrative side (Gaussian-vs-skew-normal, single-vs-nine channels, no covariate slope) and on the action-side direction emission (Bernoulli sell vs Categorical $\{-1, 0, +1\}$).

The two DEVIATION rows are the action-channel availability prior (whose hard-coded values do not match the generator's `T`-state rate) and the transition kernel (probability-space Dirichlet smoothing instead of logit-space hierarchical Gaussian). The first is a quantitative miscalibration internal to the harness; the second is a structural simplification that conflates "no hierarchy" with "Dirichlet-smoothed per-executive fit."

## Implication for what v0 validation demonstrates

The v0 harness validates that a two-state HMM with a Gaussian emission, a Bernoulli direction emission, a Bernoulli opportunistic-flag emission, and an informative-absence factor — with K=10 multi-start EM and a Dirichlet-smoothed transition — recovers known synthetic ground truth at the published threshold table. It does not validate that the §4.1-committed specification — with skew-normal nine-channel narrative emissions, the full $g_\delta \cdot g_s \cdot g_\kappa \cdot g_o \cdot g_\pi$ action decomposition, and the hierarchical Type-II ML outer loop — does so. The two are different models. The v1 harness expansion is the work item that closes the gap; until it exists, the design document's §11 limitations subsection states the gap explicitly.
